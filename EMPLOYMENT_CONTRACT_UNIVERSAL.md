# Employment Contract
*by The Lindemans, inc.*

> This contract is governed by the company’s bylaws in `BYLAWS.md`. Bracketed references like [Bylaws IV.4] point to the controlling section. If this contract and the bylaws ever differ, the bylaws control as of their effective date [Bylaws VIII.2].

## Section I: Position and Duties

1. **Role Assignment**  
   Employees are assigned roles based on expertise and company needs. The company is flat; all employees report to the **President** for day-to-day management, while the **People Lead** and **Compliance Lead** provide oversight without direct reports [Bylaws II.2, II.3, II.4; III.1–III.2]. The President holds that office as a secondary obligation and also works a needed operational role [Bylaws II.2]. Roles can be adjusted as the company grows [Bylaws IV.1].

2. **Work Hours**  
   **All employees are full-time**; there are no part-time positions [Bylaws IV.4]. Required minimum is 16 hours per week across three or more days and attendance at the all-hands on Mondays at 9 AM Pacific Time, with recordings and alternative channels available [Bylaws IV.4]. Weekly allocation minimums: 8 hours on assigned role duties and 8 hours on approved company projects; additional hours are at employee discretion and interest [Bylaws IV.4]. These are minimums only; no employee will keep another waiting on a blocking task for more than 24 hours measured in the recipient’s working calendar, and each day an employee works they clear all tasks due before their next scheduled workday [Bylaws IV.4].

3. **Projects, Teams, and Nonprofit Service**  
   Employees may opt into projects beyond formal roles; projects must align with the mission and can evolve into teams by vote [Bylaws IV.2–IV.3]. Employees may allocate paid working time to the 501(c)(3) subsidiary under an approved service grant; that time counts as company service for all purposes in these bylaws [Bylaws I.4, IV.4].

## Section II: Compensation and Benefits

1. **Equal Pay and Payroll Funding**  
   All employees receive identical pay. Total payroll for the fiscal year equals 50 percent of prior-year profit and is divided evenly among full-time employees; pay is issued on the regular schedule posted in the governance repository [Bylaws V.1, V.3].

2. **Equity and Voting**  
   Each quarter the company calculates **Allotted Shares** for every employee as that employee’s cumulative time divided by the sum of all employees’ cumulative time. **Voting Shares** per employee equal the smallest Allotted Shares number across employees at that calculation, which makes voting one person one vote. **Non-Voting Equity Units** equal Allotted Shares minus Voting Shares; they have no dividends and no voting rights. On separation for any reason, all units are returned and cancelled [Bylaws V.2; VII.2].

3. **No Profit Distributions; Long-term Reserve**  
   The company does not distribute profits. The remaining 50 percent of prior-year profit funds a long-term investment reserve with the objective of decade-scale gains sufficient to fund a following year’s payroll, withdrawn only under a payroll policy approved by vote [Bylaws V.4].

4. **Charitable Revenue Sharing**  
   The company donates 1 percent of prior-year GAAP revenue to qualified charities aligned with the public-benefit purpose; recipients are disclosed in the governance repository [Bylaws V.5].

5. **Pension**  
   After 20 years of cumulative company service, an employee may retire and receive a pension equal to the highest annual equal-pay salary from any of the five full fiscal years immediately preceding retirement, paid in equal weekly installments. Pension obligations are treated as operating costs and do not change the equal-pay calculation [Bylaws V.6].

6. **Tuition Reimbursement**  
   Unlimited full reimbursement of accredited college tuition for employees, spouses, and dependent children. Tuition means billed tuition charges; other fees are covered where required for enrollment. Direct pay to institutions when practical or reimbursement on invoice [Bylaws V.7].

7. **Child Care Support**  
   Full coverage of qualified child care costs for dependent children, with direct pay to licensed providers when practical or reimbursement on itemized invoice [Bylaws V.8].

## Section III: Performance and Reviews

1. **Learning and Feedback**  
   Employees pursue continuous improvement through peer and 360-degree reviews with the President and teammates. Reviews are for growth and do not determine employment decisions [Bylaws IV.7; II.5; VI.2 records context].

## Section IV: Disputes and Separation

1. **Dispute Resolution**  
   The People Lead handles disputes through mediation, documentation, and clear escalation paths that protect against retaliation and respect confidentiality; employees may bring advocates, submit anonymous concerns, propose systemic fixes, and appeal to the President [Bylaws VII.1].

2. **Supportive Separation**  
   The company uses a supportive path: clarify expectations and give specific feedback; offer supports such as mentorship, workload adjustment, or training; explore role redesign or project transfer; if misalignment persists, offer mutual separation with fair severance bands. Immediate separation can occur for safety, harassment, illegality, or severe policy violations. Retaliation is prohibited [Bylaws VII.2].

3. **Return of Equity on Separation**  
   When employment ends, all shares and equity units are returned to the company and cancelled as of the separation date [Bylaws V.2; VII.2].

## Section V: Confidentiality and IP

1. **Confidentiality**  
   Employees will keep proprietary information and trade secrets confidential. Unauthorized disclosure can result in immediate separation consistent with the supportive-separation framework [Bylaws VII.2; III.4 exceptions to transparency].

2. **Intellectual Property and Licensing**  
   All work created by employees as part of their role is the exclusive property of the company. Licensing follows the bylaws: MPLv2 for default source code; CC0-1.0 for governance documents; proprietary rights reserved for paywalled features, machine learning source code, and software executables; nonprofit maintenance of released open-source is permitted under service grants [Bylaws IX.1–IX.4; I.4].

## Section VI: Amendments and Alignment with Bylaws

1. **Automatic Alignment with Bylaws**  
   This contract will automatically update to remain consistent with the bylaws. When a bylaw amendment is approved and merged under the voting procedures [Bylaws VI.1–VI.2; VIII.1], any conflicting contract term is superseded as of the bylaws’ effective date trigger [Bylaws VIII.2]. No separate signed amendment is required for that alignment.

2. **Mutual Amendments Beyond Alignment**  
   Changes that add terms not covered by the bylaws require mutual written agreement between the employee and the company. Posted equal-pay amounts, calculation dates, voter rolls, and benefit forms referenced here can be updated in the governance repository without amending this contract, provided they follow the bylaws [Bylaws V.1–V.8; VI.2; V.3].

3. **Notice**  
   The company provides notice of bylaw changes and aligned updates to this contract by posting in the governance repository and linking from the relevant pull request and vote record [Bylaws VI.2; V.3].
