# Employee Handbook
*by The Lindemans, inc.*

> This handbook is governed by `BYLAWS.md`. Bracketed references like [Bylaws II.5] point to the controlling section. If this handbook and the bylaws ever differ, the bylaws control as of their effective date [Bylaws VIII.2].

---

## Section I: Company Culture and Values

1. **Empowerment and Flexibility**  
   Employees share in decision making, with transparency and documented reasoning; authority exists to serve empowerment and the mission [Bylaws II.5; I.2; III.4].

2. **Collaborative Environment**  
   Peer collaboration is the default. Employees may opt into projects outside formal roles to spark innovation and shared ownership [Bylaws IV.2; III.3].

3. **Transparency and Open Communication**  
   Financials, project metrics, strategy, and decisions are visible to employees, with narrow exceptions for personal matters and legal constraints [Bylaws III.4]. Accessibility of tools and docs is a standing requirement [Bylaws II.7].

4. **Core Values and Measures**  
   We practice supportive behavior, curiosity and learning, empathy and advocacy, leadership and transformation, and transparency and simplicity; each value has concrete evidence expectations in planning and review rituals [Bylaws II.6].

5. **Inclusion and Humane Design**  
   We follow our DEI commitments, include a phonetic name field on all job applications, aim to follow BCG personalization best practices where consistent with privacy and public benefit, and run a pre-launch humane risk check that documents mitigations [Bylaws X.2; X.7].

---

## Section II: Dispute Resolution

1. **Resolution Procedures**  
   The People Lead mediates disputes using a documented process that protects against retaliation, respects confidentiality, and allows anonymous concerns and advocates; decisions can be appealed to the President [Bylaws VII.1; II.3; II.2].

2. **Process**  
   Direct conversation comes first; if unresolved, request mediation by the People Lead; if still unresolved, escalate to the President. Systemic issues may be addressed by proposing bylaw amendments for vote [Bylaws VII.1; VI.1–VI.2; VIII.1].

---

## Section III: Team Dynamics and Decision Making

1. **Team Collaboration**  
   Teams operate with shared responsibility and peer-driven decision making; contributors coordinate without a hierarchy while reporting to the President in the flat structure [Bylaws III.1; III.3].

2. **Leadership Support**  
   The President provides day-to-day coordination as a secondary obligation while also working an operational role; People Lead and Compliance Lead provide oversight without direct reports [Bylaws II.2–II.4; III.2].

3. **Conflict Resolution in Teams**  
   Address conflicts directly, then follow the dispute process if needed [Bylaws VII.1].

---

## Section IV: Innovation and Project Participation

1. **Project Creation**  
   Any employee may propose projects that align with the mission; proposals state objectives, resources, and impact [Bylaws IV.3; I.2].

2. **Project Approval and Funding**  
   Employee shareholders vote on proposals using the governance repository; approved projects receive time and resources within the allocation norms [Bylaws IV.3; VI.2; IV.4].

3. **Project to Team Transition**  
   Projects with sustained strategic value or profitability can become formal teams after President review and a shareholder vote [Bylaws IV.3].

4. **Nonprofit Service**  
   Employees may allocate paid working time to the 501(c)(3) subsidiary through a service grant; that time counts as company service for all bylaw purposes [Bylaws I.4; IV.4].

---

## Section V: Performance and Learning

1. **Quarterly 360-Degree Reviews**  
   Teammates and the President hold quarterly 360s to support growth; reviews provide feedback and do not drive employment decisions [Bylaws IV.7].

2. **Continuous Feedback**  
   Learning goals, post-mortems, and quarterly learning notes are expected; proposals include a one-page summary to support simple, transparent choices [Bylaws II.6; II.5].

---

## Section VI: Workplace Policies

1. **Attendance and Minimums**  
   All employees are full-time. Minimum is 16 hours per week across three or more days. Attend the all-hands each Monday at 9 AM Pacific Time; recordings and alternate channels are provided [Bylaws IV.4].

2. **Responsiveness**  
   These are minimums only. Do not keep a colleague waiting more than 24 hours on a blocking task measured in the recipient’s working calendar. On each day you work, clear tasks due between that day and your next scheduled workday [Bylaws IV.4].

3. **Remote Work and Accessibility**  
   Remote and flexible work are supported; tools and documentation must be accessible to employees of varying abilities and formats [Bylaws II.7; III.4].

4. **Health and Safety**  
   Maintain a safe and healthy work environment and follow applicable law and policy [Bylaws II.4; II.7].

5. **Code of Conduct**  
   Act with respect, inclusion, and integrity. Harassment, discrimination, or severe policy violations can lead to immediate separation consistent with the supportive-separation framework [Bylaws X.1; X.8; VII.2].

6. **Hiring Notes**  
   Job applications must include a field for phonetic name pronunciation. Hiring is by employee-shareholder vote following President selection of candidates within approved budgets [Bylaws X.2; II.2; VI.1].

## Section VII: Compensation and Benefits

1. **Equal Pay**  
   Everyone is paid the same. Total payroll equals fifty percent of prior-year profit and is divided evenly among full-time employees; pay is issued on the posted schedule [Bylaws V.1; V.3].

2. **Equity and Voting**  
   Each quarter the company calculates Allotted Shares by cumulative time. Each employee’s Voting Shares equal the smallest Allotted Shares across employees at that calculation which yields one person one vote. Non-Voting Equity Units equal Allotted Shares minus Voting Shares and carry no dividends. On separation, all units return to the company and are cancelled [Bylaws V.2; VII.2].

3. **No Profit Distributions; Investment Reserve**  
   No dividends are paid. The remaining fifty percent of prior-year profit funds a long-term reserve intended to cover a future year of payroll from realized gains each decade, withdrawn only under the payroll policy approved by vote [Bylaws V.4].

4. **Charitable Revenue Sharing**  
   The company donates one percent of prior-year GAAP revenue to qualified charities aligned with the public-benefit purpose and discloses recipients in the governance repository [Bylaws V.5].

5. **Pension**  
   After twenty years of cumulative company service, an employee may retire and receive a weekly pension equal to the highest annual equal-pay salary from any of the five full fiscal years immediately before retirement; pensions are operating costs and do not alter equal-pay calculations [Bylaws V.6].

6. **Tuition Reimbursement**  
   Unlimited full reimbursement of accredited college tuition for employees, spouses, and dependent children; direct pay when practical or reimbursement on invoice; tuition means billed tuition charges and required enrollment fees [Bylaws V.7].

7. **Child Care Support**  
   Full coverage of qualified child care costs for dependent children; direct pay to licensed providers when practical or reimbursement on itemized invoice [Bylaws V.8].

## Section VIII: Amendment Process for the Handbook

1. **Automatic Alignment with Bylaws**  
   This handbook automatically updates to remain consistent with the bylaws. When a bylaw amendment is approved and merged under the voting procedures, any conflicting handbook text is superseded as of the bylaws’ effective date trigger; no separate approval is required for that alignment [Bylaws VI.1–VI.2; VIII.1–VIII.2].

2. **Improvement Flow**  
   Employees propose improvements through the governance repository. Use pull requests with a one-page summary, impact analysis, and discussion. Votes are open-ended until the passage rule is met or the proposal is withdrawn or closed by a separate motion [Bylaws II.6; VI.1–VI.2].

3. **Record Keeping**  
   The governance repository holds vote records, posted pay schedules, equity worksheets, pension disclosures, and beneficiary disclosures for charitable giving [Bylaws V.3; V.6; V.5; VI.2].

## Summary

This handbook is a practical guide that ties daily work to the company’s values and rules. It focuses on empowerment, transparency, peer collaboration, and humane design while mapping every routine to a bylaw for clarity and accountability [Bylaws I–X].
