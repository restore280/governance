# ARTICLES OF INCORPORATION  
## OF BY THE LINDEMANS, INC.

### Article 1: Corporate Name
The name of the corporation is **by The Lindemans, inc.**.

### Article 2: Benefit Corporation; Purpose
(a) **Benefit corporation status.** This corporation **is a benefit corporation** subject to the California Benefit Corporation Law.  
(b) **General public benefit.** The corporation has the purpose of creating a general public benefit.  
(c) **Specific public benefit.** The corporation’s specific public benefit purpose is: “To empower individuals through innovative technology so that they can face their challenges with resilience to create a more hopeful world for all.”  
(d) **Stakeholder consideration.** In discharging their duties, directors shall consider the effects of any action or inaction on shareholders, employees, customers, suppliers, the community, and the environment.  
(e) **Reporting.** The corporation will prepare and deliver an annual benefit report to shareholders and make it publicly available as required by law.

### Article 5: Capitalization
(a) The corporation is authorized to issue **3,000 shares** of **common stock, no par value**.  
(b) The corporation **may issue fractional shares** or scrip and may provide for their rights or cash in lieu of fractional shares to the extent permitted by law.  
(c) **No preemptive rights.** No holder of shares shall have any preemptive, preferential, or subscription right to acquire shares, options, or securities of the corporation unless granted by a written agreement approved by the board.

### Article 6: Limitation of Director Liability
The liability of directors for monetary damages shall be eliminated to the fullest extent permitted by California law, provided that nothing herein eliminates or limits liability for acts or omissions involving intentional misconduct or knowing violations of law.

### Article 7: Indemnification
The corporation shall indemnify its directors, officers, employees, and agents to the fullest extent permitted by law and may enter into indemnification agreements and purchase insurance.
