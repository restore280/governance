# DOCUMENT RETENTION AND DESTRUCTION POLICY
## restore280 Institute

*Adopted by the Board of Directors by Written Consent, effective June 23, 2026*

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes minimum retention periods for corporate records, protects records that may be relevant to litigation or government investigation, and authorizes destruction of records whose retention period has expired. Proper document retention supports the Corporation's legal compliance, financial accountability, and institutional memory.

---

## 2. Suspension of Destruction

No records may be destroyed if the Corporation has received notice of, or reasonably anticipates, any litigation, government investigation, audit, or similar proceeding to which those records may be relevant. The Executive Director shall notify all staff and directors of any such suspension immediately upon becoming aware of the triggering circumstance.

---

## 3. Retention Schedule

**Permanent (never destroy):**

- Certificate of Incorporation and all amendments
- Bylaws and all amendments
- Board-adopted companion policies and all amendments
- Records of all Board actions (written consents and synchronous meeting records)
- Annual conflict of interest disclosures
- IRS determination letter and all IRS correspondence regarding exempt status
- Audited financial statements and audit reports
- Federal and state tax returns (Form 990 and state equivalents)
- Grant agreements and major donor agreements

**Seven years:**

- Bank statements and reconciliations
- General ledger and accounts payable/receivable records
- Contracts and agreements (from date of expiration or termination)
- Invoices and receipts
- Payroll records and employment tax filings
- Lobbying expenditure records

**Three years:**

- General correspondence not related to a specific contract or legal matter
- Non-permanent vendor records

**Until superseded:**

- Insurance policies (retain until the policy period has fully closed, including any tail coverage period)
- Registered agent records (retain current information permanently; superseded records for three years)

---

## 4. Electronic Records

Electronic records are subject to the same retention periods as paper records. Deletion of electronic files must be performed in a manner that renders the data unrecoverable. Backups and cloud storage copies must be included in any retention or destruction action.

---

## 5. Responsibility

The Executive Director is responsible for implementing this policy, maintaining an inventory of record locations, ensuring retention periods are observed, and overseeing authorized destruction. The Executive Director shall report to the Board any situation requiring suspension of destruction under Section 2.

---

## 6. Review

The Board shall review this policy at least every two years as part of the companion policy review required by Bylaws Section 10.3.
