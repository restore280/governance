# WHISTLEBLOWER PROTECTION POLICY
## restore280 Institute

*Adopted by the Board of Directors by Written Consent, effective June 23, 2026*

---

## 1. Purpose

restore280 Institute is committed to lawful and ethical conduct. This policy encourages directors, officers, employees, and volunteers to report suspected violations of law or organizational policy without fear of retaliation, and establishes a process for handling such reports.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all directors, officers, employees, contractors, and volunteers of the Corporation.

---

## 3. Reporting

Any person covered by this policy who has a good-faith concern that the Corporation or any of its directors, officers, or employees has engaged in, or is engaging in, conduct that is illegal, violates organizational policy, or constitutes financial misconduct, misuse of assets, or misrepresentation to donors or government agencies, should report that concern promptly.

**Reports should be directed to the Board Chair.** If the concern involves the Board Chair, the report should be directed to any other Independent Director.

Reports may be made in writing or verbally. Anonymous reports are accepted; however, providing contact information allows for follow-up and may assist the investigation.

---

## 4. Protection Against Retaliation

No director, officer, employee, contractor, or volunteer who in good faith reports a concern under this policy shall be subject to dismissal, demotion, suspension, harassment, or any other form of retaliation. Any person who retaliates against a good-faith reporter is subject to disciplinary action up to and including removal from their role with the Corporation.

This protection applies regardless of whether the reported concern is ultimately found to be valid, provided the report was made in good faith and not as a knowing misrepresentation.

---

## 5. Confidentiality

The Corporation will handle reports under this policy with as much confidentiality as is consistent with conducting a thorough investigation. The identity of the reporting person will not be disclosed except as required by law or as necessary to conduct the investigation.

---

## 6. Investigation

The Board Chair, or the Independent Director receiving the report if the concern involves the Board Chair, shall promptly review the concern and determine whether an investigation is warranted. If so, the Board Chair shall designate one or more Independent Directors to conduct the investigation. The investigation shall be documented, and findings shall be reported to the full Board.

If the concern involves a potential violation of law, the Board shall consult legal counsel and take appropriate action, which may include reporting to government authorities.

---

## 7. False Reports

This policy does not protect persons who make reports known to be false or who make reports with malicious intent. Such conduct may itself be subject to disciplinary action.

---

## 8. Review

The Board shall review this policy at least every two years as part of the companion policy review required by Bylaws Section 10.3.
